BREAK-OUT GAME

Breakout is a classic arcade video game originally released by Atari in 1976. The game involves controlling a paddle at the bottom of the screen to bounce a ball and break through rows of bricks at the top of the screen. The objective is to clear all the bricks while avoiding missing the ball with the paddle.

Breakout was designed by Atari co-founder Nolan Bushnell and Steve Jobs, who worked on the game as a contractor before founding Apple Computer. The game was a commercial success and helped popularize the use of microprocessors in arcade games.
